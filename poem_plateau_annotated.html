Excruciating

The poem opens at maximum signal — then immediately names it as dullness. In ML loss landscapes, a model in a flat region still registers the same cost; the excruciating part is that the pain has no gradient to follow out of it.

Gambles always won

When every risk is absorbed without consequence — neither reward nor sharp loss — you are not winning. You are in a zero-gradient zone. The plateau masquerades as safety.

10,000 hours

Gladwell's threshold for mastery — but here it arrives hollow. The loss has converged to zero. The model is trained. Yet no heroism follows because convergence on the plateau is still flatness: you've minimized loss without finding meaning.

Edge of the valley

The valley is where loss is steep, felt, alive. Its mouth is where that gradient ends. To exit is to enter the plateau — where the old signals no longer reach, and no tree falls loud enough for anyone to hear.

Echo / signal

Gradients are signals. On the plateau they vanish — not because nothing happened, but because the terrain won't carry them. The echo is the loss you still feel; the plateau calls it noise and cancels it.

Noise cancelling

Active noise cancellation works by generating an inverse sound wave. The plateau doesn't silence grief — it produces its own counter-signal until your old frequency can no longer be distinguished from the background. Still it screams.

Vocals hardened into glare

Sound that cannot travel outward turns inward. The valley's emotional signal — once voice, once cry — calcifies into a kind of reflected light: harsh, blinding, trapped in the skull. Memory as internal ricochet.

Choice from a rut

A rut is a valley that has been traveled so many times it no longer looks like terrain — it looks like the only path. In optimization: a local minimum so well-worn that the loss surface itself seems to confirm you should stay.

Flat but raised

The plateau and the valley share topology: both are flat regions of the loss landscape. The difference is elevation. The plateau is the valley's grief lifted — not transcended, just displaced upward, still horizontal, still stuck.

Candle snuff

A snuffer doesn't blow out a candle — it smothers it by removing oxygen. Desires on the plateau aren't extinguished dramatically. They are quietly deprived of what they need to burn.

States hardened by decay

Both political states and psychological states — the poem refuses to separate them. Institutional decay and personal stagnation share the same mechanism: the memory of loss becomes load-bearing infrastructure, holding everything in place.

Pipes bust. Iron rusts.

Infrastructure metaphors for a nervous system on the plateau. Oxidation doesn't announce itself — it accumulates invisibly until the structure fails. Dwelling too long in flat affect is the same corrosion, on the inside.

The dry well

A well is a valley, built on purpose — a deliberate descent to reach something vital. A dry well still has the shape of the valley but nothing to draw from. Next steps feel equally empty: the motion without the resource.

Remember how you fell

The paradox of the plateau: to leave it, you need the gradient information from your original descent — the very signal the plateau has been canceling. Recovery requires re-accessing the valley, not forgetting it.

Sin derived from sensation

"Derived" is calculus: sin as the derivative of sensation. On the plateau, sensation is flat — but the memory of sensation still has slope. Temptation is the gradient that remains after the loss has leveled out.

Tempest

The poem ends on turbulence — not resolution. A tempest is weather that comes after stagnation: atmospheric pressure finally releasing. Whether it destroys or clears is the question the poem refuses to answer.
