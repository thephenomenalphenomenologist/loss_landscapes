The Attractor State

A Poem in a Series of Poems About Loss Landscapes

Terms underlined in amber carry definitions. Click to read for deeper understanding of the lyric.

The wail is but a whisper when it bounced for the last time off the barrier of the canal. Tone is signal in a coat, or noise wearing signal's hat. Not knowing which is our own fault. We all must empty into the valley, with a thud that shakes the ground but not the sky.

· · ·

And the valley is our home, and we are local to the valley and it is local to us.

· · ·

The terrain shifts beneath our feet; feet that have neither stepped up nor fallen down, still inherit the land of the world. It's the static that lulls us into a false sense of stillness.

· · ·

If the globe were not so slippery, we would live and die by the valley. Everything we can ever need gathers in the valley.

· · ·

Coming of age begins in the valley. And many would tell tall tales of peaks that we can see, Although warned not to seek. Adventure is equally the chased and the chaser.

· · ·

The ground is an empire; it may neither rise nor fall. It produces our states.

· · ·

Even the rodents of the sky who wish to fly, do not feel the need to learn the right already earned. They overshoot the valley, because we do not live and die by the gully, and are thus, overkill.

· · ·

The momentum of growth first added to their stillness, pent up energy urges a step too large later, lost to Death's wager.

· · ·

Damping the swing of here and not here left them in fear of never leaving the nest.

· · ·

The loss is alarmed by distress signals, just noise to the untrained ear.

· · ·

We all must empty into the valley, with a thud that shakes the ground but not the sky.
